BETWEEN:

(1) CloudVault Systems Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Provider")
    Address: 1200 Tech Park Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Service" means the Provider's cloud-based document management and compliance platform, known as "CloudVault Enterprise," including all updates, upgrades, and patches made available during the Subscription Term.

1.2 "Subscription Term" means the initial term of twelve (12) months commencing on the Effective Date, and each successive renewal term as described in Section 8.

1.3 "Users" means the Customer's employees, contractors, and authorized agents who are granted access to the Service under a valid user license.

1.4 "Customer Data" means all data, content, files, and information uploaded, stored, or processed through the Service by or on behalf of the Customer.

1.5 "Service Level Agreement" or "SLA" means the service availability and performance commitments set forth in Exhibit A attached hereto.

1.6 "Fees" means the subscription fees, usage-based charges, and any additional fees as set forth in Exhibit B.

1.7 "Permitted Use" means use of the Service solely for the Customer's internal business operations, including document management, regulatory compliance tracking, and audit preparation.

2. GRANT OF RIGHTS

2.1 License Grant. Subject to the terms of this Agreement and payment of applicable Fees, the Provider grants to the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, limited right to access and use the Service during the Subscription Term for the Permitted Use.

2.2 User Limits. The Customer's subscription includes up to two hundred fifty (250) named user licenses. Additional user licenses may be purchased at the then-current per-user rate specified in Exhibit B.

2.3 Restrictions. The Customer shall not:
(a) Sublicense, sell, resell, lease, or otherwise make the Service available to any third party;
(b) Modify, adapt, translate, or create derivative works based on the Service;
(c) Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Service or attempt to access its source code;
(d) Use the Service to store or transmit malicious code, infringing content, or any material that violates applicable law;
(e) Interfere with or disrupt the integrity or performance of the Service;
(f) Access the Service for competitive analysis or to build a competing product.

3. CUSTOMER DATA AND PRIVACY

3.1 Ownership. The Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer Data. The Provider acquires no rights in Customer Data except the limited rights necessary to perform the Service.

3.2 Data Processing. The Provider shall process Customer Data solely to provide the Service and shall not use Customer Data for any other purpose, including but not limited to marketing, advertising, or training machine learning models.

3.3 Data Security. The Provider shall implement and maintain industry-standard administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect Customer Data, including:
(a) AES-256 encryption for data at rest;
(b) TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit;
(c) Multi-factor authentication for administrative access;
(d) Annual SOC 2 Type II audits;
(e) Regular penetration testing by qualified third parties.

3.4 Data Breach Notification. In the event of a security incident involving unauthorized access to Customer Data, the Provider shall:
(a) Notify the Customer within seventy-two (72) hours of discovering the incident;
(b) Provide a detailed written report within ten (10) business days;
(c) Take immediate remedial action to mitigate the impact;
(d) Cooperate with the Customer's investigation and regulatory obligations.

3.5 Data Location. Customer Data shall be stored and processed exclusively within data centers located in the United States, unless otherwise agreed in writing. The Provider shall not transfer Customer Data to any jurisdiction outside the United States without the Customer's prior written consent.

3.6 Data Portability. Upon request, the Provider shall make Customer Data available for export in a standard, machine-readable format (CSV, JSON, or XML) within fifteen (15) business days.

4. SERVICE LEVELS AND SUPPORT

4.1 Availability. The Provider shall maintain a monthly uptime of at least 99.9% for the Service, measured as set forth in the SLA (Exhibit A). Scheduled maintenance windows shall not be counted as downtime, provided the Provider gives at least forty-eight (48) hours' advance notice.

4.2 Service Credits. If the Provider fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment:
(a) 99.0% - 99.89% uptime: 10% credit of the monthly Fee;
(b) 95.0% - 98.99% uptime: 25% credit of the monthly Fee;
(c) Below 95.0% uptime: 50% credit of the monthly Fee;
(d) Three consecutive months below 99.0%: Customer may terminate without penalty per Section 8.3.

4.3 Support. The Provider shall provide:
(a) 24/7 emergency support for Severity 1 issues (complete service outage);
(b) Business hours support (8 AM - 8 PM ET, Monday-Friday) for Severity 2-3 issues;
(c) A dedicated Customer Success Manager;
(d) Response times as defined in the SLA.

5. FEES AND PAYMENT

5.1 Subscription Fees. The Customer shall pay annual subscription Fees of one hundred eighty thousand dollars ($180,000), payable in advance within thirty (30) days of invoice.

5.2 Usage Fees. Storage in excess of 5 terabytes (TB) shall be billed at $0.15 per GB per month. API calls in excess of one million (1,000,000) per month shall be billed at $0.002 per call.

5.3 Fee Increases. The Provider may increase Fees for renewal terms by providing at least ninety (90) days' written notice prior to the start of the renewal term. Fee increases shall not exceed five percent (5%) per year.

5.4 Late Payment. Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less. The Provider may suspend Service access if payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue, after providing ten (10) days' written notice.

5.5 Taxes. All Fees are exclusive of taxes. The Customer shall be responsible for all sales, use, and other taxes arising from this Agreement, excluding taxes based on the Provider's net income.

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 Provider IP. The Provider retains all right, title, and interest in the Service, including all underlying software, algorithms, interfaces, documentation, and any improvements or modifications thereto.

6.2 Feedback. If the Customer provides suggestions, enhancement requests, or other feedback regarding the Service, the Provider may use such feedback without restriction or obligation to the Customer.

6.3 Aggregated Data. The Provider may collect and use aggregated, anonymized usage data derived from the Customer's use of the Service for purposes of improving the Service, provided such data does not identify the Customer or any individual User.

8. TERM, RENEWAL, AND TERMINATION

8.1 Initial Term. The initial Subscription Term is twelve (12) months from the Effective Date.

8.2 Automatic Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month terms unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

8.3 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice if the other Party:
(a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving notice;
(b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases operations;
(c) Fails to meet service level commitments for three (3) consecutive months (applicable to Customer's right only).

8.4 Termination for Convenience. The Customer may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days' written notice, subject to payment of an early termination fee equal to fifty percent (50%) of the remaining Fees for the then-current term.

8.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration:
(a) The Customer's access to the Service shall cease;
(b) The Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for thirty (30) days;
(c) After the 30-day export period, the Provider shall securely delete all Customer Data;
(d) Accrued payment obligations, confidentiality, indemnification, and limitation of liability provisions shall survive.

9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 Cap. EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF SECTION 3 (CUSTOMER DATA AND PRIVACY) OR SECTION 10 (INDEMNIFICATION), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY THE CUSTOMER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

9.2 Exclusion. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, REVENUE, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY.

9.3 Data Breach Liability. Notwithstanding Section 9.1, the Provider's liability for breaches of data security obligations under Section 3 shall not exceed two times (2x) the annual Fees.

10. INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 Provider Indemnification. The Provider shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Customer from any third-party claim that the Service infringes any patent, copyright, or trade secret, provided the Customer:
(a) Promptly notifies the Provider of the claim;
(b) Grants the Provider sole control of the defense and settlement;
(c) Provides reasonable cooperation.

10.2 Customer Indemnification. The Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Provider from any third-party claim arising from:
(a) Customer Data or the Customer's use of the Service in violation of this Agreement;
